# Landing-Page Conversion Patterns: Local Trades and Retail

Vertical: contractors, roofers, HVAC, plumbers, clinics, dentists, real estate, fitness studios, local retail.
Core insight: this vertical is proof-first and speed-to-offer. Home-service landing pages convert 5-15 percent vs 1-3 percent for homepages. Visitors decide on trust signals, not brand story. Patterns only, sourced below. Researched 2026-07-03.

## 1. Section archetypes that convert (canonical order)

1. **Hero with embedded offer + phone** (`hero`). Headline names service + area + outcome. Tap-to-call and a short quote form both visible above the fold, license number and insured line in the hero itself. Pages offering only form OR phone lose one buyer class (emergency callers vs deliberate shoppers).
2. **Trust strip** (`references`, first instance, kept thin). Multi-platform review bar directly under the hero: Google stars + count, BBB, license/insured/bonded badges, manufacturer certs. Trust sits where doubt lives, next to headline and CTA, never in the footer.
3. **Problem framed locally and seasonally** (`problem`). Ties the pain to geography and weather or life event, not abstract pain. Short.
4. **How it works, 3-4 steps** (`process`, optionally `system`). Detailed process explanations lift time-on-page up to 65 percent because they de-risk the unknown of letting a stranger into the house. Step 1 is always the free, low-commitment thing.
5. **Before/after gallery** (`portfolio`, `output`). Real jobs, real addresses-adjacent captions (neighborhood, job type). Dentists call it a smile gallery. This is the single strongest proof block in the vertical.
6. **Named local reviews** (`references`, full section). 3-5 reviews with first name, neighborhood, date, ideally headshot. Volume claim across platforms ("999+ across Google, Facebook, BBB" style).
7. **Single clear offer** (`packages` -> `checkout`). One offer per page, not a service menu. Reframe "free estimate" with specificity (itemized inspection + photo report). Financing widget where ticket size justifies it.
8. **FAQ, 4-6 objections** (`faq`). Price, timeline, insurance, warranty, mess/disruption.
9. **Final CTA repeating phone + form** (`finalCta`). Sticky mobile call bar throughout.

Cut list: winners run 6-8 sections, not 10+. Long pages dilute urgency for time-sensitive visitors. Sub-2.5s mobile LCP is table stakes (60-70 percent of this traffic is mobile).

## 2. Three hero patterns

- **Crew-photo split hero.** Left 55-60 percent: offer-led headline (service + area + guarantee), one trust line (licensed, bonded, insured, license no.), phone button + short form. Right: real uniformed crew or technician at a real job site, not stock. Type scale moderate (headline ~2.5-3.5rem), because the phone number and form compete for attention and must win.
- **Full-bleed job photo with overlay panel.** Completed roof/renovation/premises photo full width, dark scrim, headline centered or left, quote form floats as a card over the image (desktop right, mobile below). Review bar pinned at the panel's base. Suits roofing, remodel, real estate.
- **Text-first guarantee hero.** No hero photo, centered oversized promise headline plus guarantee line, phone mnemonic huge, trust badges in a row beneath (Happy Hiller pattern). Works when the brand voice and guarantee are the differentiator; imagery moves into the gallery section.

Headlines are offer-led, not brand-led: outcome + timeframe + risk-reversal ("done in a day", "free for 7 days" shapes). Never lead with the company name.

## 3. Trust and social-proof patterns (local-specific)

- Google review stars + real count, ideally live-synced, shown in hero and repeated at the offer.
- Triple-platform bar: Google + Yelp/Facebook + BBB grade, one thin row below the headline.
- Licensed / insured / bonded badges with the actual license number visible without scrolling.
- Years-in-business and volume stats ("since 1990", installs count, employee tenure).
- Service-area block: named suburbs/neighborhoods list or simple map, doubles as local SEO.
- Before/after gallery with neighborhood-level captions.
- Named local testimonials: first name + area + date, headshots beat initials (harder to fake).
- Manufacturer/insurer partnerships (cert programs, "preferred by" carrier logos), staff bios with credentials for clinics.
- Guarantee stated as a sentence, not an icon ("service is free if..." shapes).

## 4. Copy register

- Tone: plain, direct, benefit + speed. Reassuring for clinics, energetic for fitness, matter-of-fact for trades. Second person throughout.
- Headlines: 6-10 words. Subhead one sentence handling the top objection.
- CTA verbs: "Get a free quote", "Call now", "Book an appointment", "Schedule your inspection", "Book my free week", "Get my free estimate". First-person possessive lifts form CTAs.
- One dominant CTA per page (call or form primary, the other secondary), repeated 3-4 times.
- Avoid: "solutions", "world-class", "synergy", trade jargon (SEER ratings, torch-down, occlusal) unless immediately translated, and any superlative without a number behind it.

## 5. Imagery brief

Subjects: crew at work on real jobs, owner/staff portraits, premises exterior and interior, before/after pairs, finished results, real customers (with consent). Photographic always, illustrative only for process icons. Ratios: hero 16:9 or 3:2, before/after pairs 1:1 or 4:3 side by side, portraits 4:5, gallery 4:3 grid.

- **Premium-agency (cinematic full-bleed, Geist):** one hero-grade full-bleed job photo, graded and dramatic, crew small in frame; everything else restrained.
- **Editorial-minimal (warm monochrome, serif, hairlines):** documentary black-and-white or muted crew photography, generous margins, before/after as captioned plates.
- **Bold-brutalist (Swiss print, uppercase, 2px rules):** high-contrast flash-lit job photos in strict grid cells, stats set as oversized type instead of decorative imagery.
- **Warm-boutique (creams, rounded, soft shadows):** natural-light staff and premises photos, rounded-corner cards, best fit for clinics, dentists, fitness.
- **Dark-cinematic (OLED dark, glass, serif):** night/dusk premises and equipment shots, glass cards over dark imagery; keep phone/CTA contrast very high, suits high-ticket remodel and real estate.

## 6. Anti-patterns and AI-slop tells

- Generic stock: handshake, hardhat-pointing-at-blueprint, too-white dental smile stock, empty gym renders.
- Fake or unverifiable review counts, five stars with no platform or count, testimonial cards with stock avatars.
- "24/7 emergency service" or "same-day" claims with no basis in the actual business.
- Over-designed minimalism that hides the phone number or buries the CTA below scroll art.
- Service-menu pages that sell everything at once instead of one offer per page.
- Ten-plus sections, autoplay carousels, hero video that delays LCP past 2.5s.
- Vague guarantees, "trusted by thousands" with no proof artifact, missing license number.
- Em-dashes, "Elevate your...", "seamless", "unlock", symmetric three-icon feature rows with meaningless icons.
