# Funding Program Offer Positioning

This document provides copy direction for selling the Funding Program module and its white-label versions. Use it for landing pages, outbound campaigns, discovery calls, partner pitches, and proposal framing.

## Core Positioning

Businesses do not need another vague list of grants. They need a fundable growth project: a clear objective, practical scope, credible budget, implementation plan, and application-ready story.

The Funding Program module helps B2B prospects move from "could we get funding?" to "what project should we prepare, and what evidence do we need?"

## Primary Offer Statement

Find the strongest funding-backed growth path before you spend on applications, agencies, or software.

We screen your business context, identify likely growth lanes, and shape the next step into a fundable project plan for digital modernization, e-commerce, export marketing, automation, training, procurement, clean technology, creative technology, or market expansion.

## Short Copy Blocks

### Hero Copy

Find out if your business may qualify for funding-backed digital growth.

Start with a focused scan of your business, goals, budget, and digital needs. We will review likely funding lanes and recommend whether a blueprint, application support, execution scope, or monitoring rhythm makes sense.

### Problem Copy

Funding is hard to act on when the project is still fuzzy. Most businesses need the project shape before the application: scope, budget, milestones, outcomes, and the operational plan to deliver it.

### Process Copy

Start with eligibility signals. Then build the project.

The Funding Fit Scan captures your business profile, growth goals, current systems, and budget range. From there, we review the strongest lane, identify missing evidence, and recommend the right next step.

### Disclaimer Copy

The Funding Fit Scan is not a grant application and does not guarantee eligibility, approval, timing, or funding amount. Program fit depends on the specific funding pathway, intake window, documentation, business profile, and review process.

## Outreach Angles

### Digital Modernization

Use this angle for businesses with outdated websites, manual processes, weak CRM usage, disconnected tools, or clear automation needs.

Subject ideas:

- Funding-backed digital modernization for {{businessName}}
- A project scope for {{businessName}}'s digital upgrade
- Before applying for digital funding

Message angle:

Your business may have a digital modernization project worth shaping before any application work starts. The first step is to define the operational problem, budget, milestones, and evidence needed to support a funding-backed project.

### E-Commerce Growth

Use this angle for retailers, consumer brands, food brands, beauty, apparel, or businesses already selling online.

Subject ideas:

- E-commerce growth funding fit for {{businessName}}
- Turning online sales goals into a fundable project
- Shopify, conversion, and growth project scope

Message angle:

If the goal is to improve online sales, the project needs more than a platform update. It should connect conversion, merchandising, paid acquisition, content, analytics, and implementation capacity.

### Export Marketing

Use this angle for businesses exporting already or considering U.S. or international expansion.

Subject ideas:

- Export marketing project scope for {{businessName}}
- Funding fit for market-entry work
- Building the case for export growth

Message angle:

Export-related funding conversations usually need a clear target market, market-entry plan, campaign scope, budget, and proof that the business can execute.

### Automation And CRM

Use this angle for services, manufacturing, construction, health, professional services, and teams with manual workflows.

Subject ideas:

- Automation project fit for {{businessName}}
- CRM and workflow funding readiness
- Scope before software spend

Message angle:

Automation projects are easier to support when the business can show the workflow problem, expected efficiency gain, implementation plan, training needs, and measurable outcomes.

### Workforce Training

Use this angle for employers with onboarding, enablement, upskilling, or team adoption needs.

Subject ideas:

- Training and enablement funding fit
- Upskilling project scope for {{businessName}}
- Team capability as a growth project

Message angle:

Training is strongest when tied to a specific growth project: new systems, new markets, new sales process, new operational capacity, or new delivery model.

### Public Procurement

Use this angle for businesses that sell or want to sell to government, institutions, municipalities, or public-sector buyers.

Subject ideas:

- Procurement readiness for {{businessName}}
- Supplier profile and RFP readiness project
- Public-sector sales preparation

Message angle:

Procurement readiness can be scoped as a business-development project: supplier profile, capability statement, case studies, compliance assets, market list, and RFP response process.

### Clean Tech Or Sustainability

Use this angle for climate, energy, retrofit, carbon, sustainability, manufacturing, construction, and transportation prospects.

Subject ideas:

- Clean technology commercialization readiness
- Funding fit for sustainability growth
- Scoping a clean-tech growth project

Message angle:

Clean-tech and sustainability projects need clear commercialization, adoption, or implementation evidence. The first step is to define the project outcome and the documentation required to support it.

### Market Expansion

Use this angle for businesses pursuing new segments, cities, verticals, channels, or partnerships.

Subject ideas:

- Market expansion funding fit for {{businessName}}
- Turning growth plans into a project scope
- A fundable path into new markets

Message angle:

Market expansion should be framed around target customers, channel strategy, campaigns, sales process, measurement, and implementation capacity.

## Offer Ladder Copy

### Funding Fit Scan

A free first step to capture your business profile, growth goals, project budget, digital needs, and funding-lane signals. You receive a practical recommendation for what to do next.

### Fundable Project Blueprint

A paid planning sprint that converts the opportunity into a project scope, budget assumptions, milestones, implementation plan, and application-ready narrative.

### Application Support

Hands-on support for packaging a specific project into application materials. This includes narrative, budget alignment, document checklist, review coordination, and submission support where appropriate.

### Funded Growth Execution

Delivery support for the approved or budgeted project: digital assets, campaigns, e-commerce improvements, automation, training content, market expansion systems, and measurement setup.

### Monthly Opportunity Intelligence

Ongoing monitoring and advisory support for businesses that want a monthly funding and growth-opportunity review rhythm.

## Qualification Talk Track

Use these questions on discovery calls:

- What is the business trying to change or grow in the next 6 to 12 months?
- What would this project make possible?
- What systems, channels, or assets are currently missing?
- What is already working?
- What budget is available if funding is partial, reimbursed, delayed, or unavailable?
- Who owns implementation internally?
- What documents can the business provide quickly?
- Are there specific programs in mind, or is the business still exploring?
- Is the goal planning, application support, execution, or ongoing monitoring?

### Small And Mid-Sized Businesses

Need help translating growth goals into a fundable project. They buy clarity, confidence, and execution support.

### Agencies And Consultants

Need a lead-generation and qualification front end for clients who ask about funding. They buy a white-label intake and offer ladder.

### Economic-Development And Industry Partners

Need a structured intake to triage member or local-business needs. They buy consistent qualification, reporting, and partner routing.

### Advisors And Finance Professionals

Need to identify clients with credible growth projects before deeper advisory or application work. They buy readiness screening and project packaging.

## White-Label Positioning

White-label tenants should present the offer in the partner's language while keeping the same promise:

"We help businesses identify and prepare funding-ready growth projects."

Partner-facing pitch:

Launch a branded funding-readiness funnel without building the intake, package ladder, admin review process, and outreach workflow from scratch. Your team can capture structured leads, triage funding lanes, route prospects into paid services, and keep the public promise compliant.

White-label value points:

- Branded public funnel.
- Funding-specific intake.
- Tenant-specific package ladder.
- Admin lead pipeline.
- Outreach drafts and templates.
- Partner-owned booking and payment routing.
- Repeatable workflow for qualification and proposal support.

## Copy Guardrails

Use:

- "May qualify."
- "Likely lane."
- "Funding-backed growth."
- "Readiness signals."
- "Project scope."
- "Application-ready narrative."
- "Potential pathway."
- "Recommended next step."

Avoid:

- "You qualify."
- "Guaranteed funding."
- "Approved grant."
- "We will get you money."
- "Free government money."
- "Guaranteed reimbursement."
- "Eligible for every program."
- "Official funding partner" unless legally true.

## Proposal Positioning

A good proposal should sell reduced ambiguity. The buyer is not only paying for writing. They are paying for:

- Project definition.
- Funding-pathway review.
- Budget logic.
- Evidence collection.
- Narrative development.
- Implementation planning.
- Decision support.
- Reduced risk before deeper application or delivery work.

Frame the proposal around the business outcome, not the funding program alone.
