# Funding Program Module

The Funding Program module is a white-label B2B sales funnel for businesses that want to turn growth plans into funding-ready digital projects. It does not promise eligibility, grant approval, or program matching. Its job is to capture business context, screen for likely funding-backed growth lanes, and route qualified prospects into blueprint, application support, execution, or opportunity intelligence offers.

The built-in tenant is `funded-growth`, branded as DGTL Funded Growth Studio. The public page positions the first step as a free Funding Fit Scan for Canadian businesses exploring digital modernization, e-commerce, export marketing, automation, training, procurement, clean technology, creative technology, or market expansion.

## B2B Sales Explanation

The module sells clarity before application work. Many businesses hear about grants, tax credits, accelerators, or public-sector programs before they have a fundable project scope. The Funding Program offer helps convert vague interest into a concrete business case:

- What growth outcome is the business trying to achieve?
- Which funding lane appears directionally relevant?
- What evidence, budget, team capacity, and documentation are missing?
- What project should be scoped before any program-specific application work begins?
- Which paid service is the logical next step?

The sales motion should be consultative. The lead magnet is a free scan, but the commercial objective is to qualify prospects for paid planning and implementation. The recommended path is:

1. Free Funding Fit Scan.
2. Fundable Project Blueprint.
3. Application Support, when a specific program pathway is worth pursuing.
4. Funded Growth Execution, when the business has approval, internal budget, or a strong case to move.
5. Monthly Opportunity Intelligence, when the prospect needs ongoing monitoring and prioritization.

The core message is: "Do not start with a grant application. Start with a fundable project."

## Module Goals

- Capture structured funding-readiness information from public funnel visitors.
- Rank likely growth funding lanes with deterministic, non-claiming logic.
- Give admins enough context to qualify leads and generate next-step outreach.
- Support white-label tenant versions for partners, agencies, advisors, and regional business-development teams.
- Convert qualified prospects into blueprint, application support, execution, or retainer opportunities.

## Landing Page Structure

The current public funnel is built from tenant configuration and should keep this structure:

1. **Hero**
   - Lead with the funding-backed growth promise.
   - Primary CTA: start the Funding Fit Scan.
   - Secondary CTA: view growth packages.
   - Use proof points such as number of screened lanes, free initial scan, and Canadian growth focus.

2. **Problem**
   - Explain that funding is hard to act on without a project shape.
   - Show the practical pains: unclear programs, scattered digital needs, missing scope, and grant-timeline uncertainty.

3. **System**
   - Explain the studio model: scan, blueprint, execution.
   - Position the business as moving from interest to evidence-backed project plan.

4. **Process**
   - Funding Fit Scan.
   - Lane Review.
   - Project Blueprint.
   - Application and Delivery.

5. **Funding Lanes**
   - Show the opportunity categories screened by the scan.
   - Avoid implying eligibility or approval.

6. **Packages**
   - Present the free scan first.
   - Then show paid next steps: blueprint, application support, execution, monthly intelligence.

7. **Execution Band**
   - Reinforce that the goal is a real growth project, not only an application.

8. **Intake Form**
   - Capture business identity, contact details, website, industry, location, employee count, revenue range, years operating, incorporation status, export signals, digital needs, e-commerce needs, automation needs, available budget, and main growth goal.

9. **FAQ**
   - Clarify that the scan is not an application.
   - Clarify that no funding is guaranteed.
   - Explain the next-step recommendation process.

10. **Final CTA**
    - Bring the visitor back to the free scan.

## Opportunity Categories

The module screens these opportunity categories:

- **Digital Adoption**: websites, CRM, software, automation, online systems, digital modernization.
- **Ecommerce Growth**: Shopify, conversion, online sales, paid acquisition, digital merchandising.
- **Export Marketing**: export readiness, U.S. or international market entry, trade marketing, market-specific campaigns.
- **Creative Tech**: media, content, video, AR, VR, interactive, education, software-enabled creative work.
- **Clean Tech**: sustainability, climate, energy, retrofit, carbon reduction, commercialization support.
- **Workforce Training**: hiring, onboarding, upskilling, enablement, training content, team capability building.
- **Public Procurement**: government sales, RFP readiness, supplier profile, public-sector positioning.
- **Market Expansion**: lead generation, new-market campaigns, sales systems, growth funnels, regional expansion.

These categories are sales and qualification lanes, not final program determinations.

## Qualification Criteria

Use the scan to evaluate whether the prospect has enough evidence for paid next-step work.

Strong-fit signals:

- Canadian business with clear location and operating history.
- Incorporated or actively formalizing the business.
- Revenue traction or a credible available project budget.
- Specific growth goal tied to modernization, export, e-commerce, automation, procurement, training, clean tech, creative tech, or expansion.
- Clear website, product, service, or market presence.
- Employee count and team capacity for implementation.
- Willingness to scope budget, milestones, outcomes, and documentation before applying.

Weak-fit or nurture signals:

- No clear growth goal.
- Pre-revenue with no available budget.
- No team capacity to deliver a project.
- Request is only "find me free money."
- No evidence of market traction, customer base, or implementation need.
- Unwillingness to provide documentation.

Disqualifying or caution signals:

- Prospect expects guaranteed funding.
- Prospect wants application submission without a real project.
- Prospect lacks legal or business basics required for most programs.
- Prospect needs regulated professional advice outside the module scope.
- Timeline is incompatible with funding windows or implementation capacity.

## Packages

### Funding Fit Scan

Free intake used to capture readiness signals and recommend a next step. It should not be sold as a program match or eligibility confirmation.

### Fundable Project Blueprint

Paid planning offer that turns a growth opportunity into a scoped project with budget assumptions, milestones, outcomes, implementation plan, and application-ready narrative.

### Application Support

Paid support for preparing materials around a specific program path. This can include narrative, budget alignment, document collection, submission coordination, and review support.

### Funded Growth Execution

Implementation support after the project is approved, budgeted, or otherwise ready to move. Examples include digital assets, campaigns, automation, e-commerce systems, market expansion funnels, and measurement setup.

### Monthly Opportunity Intelligence

Retainer for ongoing opportunity monitoring, monthly reviews, readiness guidance, and project prioritization.

## White-Label Tenant Use Case

The module can be repackaged for a partner tenant without changing app code. A partner can run the same workflow under its own brand, domain, CTA copy, packages, routing, and booking links.

Good white-label partners include:

- Digital agencies that want a funding-backed growth advisory front end.
- Business coaches and consultants serving SMEs.
- Regional economic-development groups.
- Industry associations.
- Accountants, fractional CFOs, and grant advisors who need better project intake.
- B2B service firms that implement digital modernization, e-commerce, automation, or export marketing projects.

Tenant configuration should adjust:

- Brand name, logo text, colors, and sender identity.
- Public domain and slug.
- Hero and package copy.
- Booking links and payment links.
- Reply-to, phone, and lead routing.
- Outreach templates and offer language.

The white-label promise should remain bounded: the tenant helps businesses identify and prepare fundable growth projects; it does not guarantee funding.

## Compliance And Positioning Boundaries

- Do not claim that the scan determines eligibility.
- Do not guarantee approval, funding amount, timeline, or reimbursement.
- Do not imply official government affiliation unless the tenant truly has it.
- Do not present generalized funding categories as live program availability.
- Do not provide legal, accounting, or regulated advice unless handled by qualified professionals.
- Keep all public copy framed around readiness, fit, project scoping, and application support.
